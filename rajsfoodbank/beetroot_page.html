Beetroot

Soil Requirements

Beetroot plants requires a slightly acidic soil of between 5.8 to 7.0ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Beetroot plants also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 15cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow beetroot seeds outdoors between March and July. Choose an area that gets partial sunlight. Prepare ground by mixing a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil.

Sow three seeds at a depth of 2.5cm and at 10cm intervals. Seeds sown from June onwards can be used for storing in winter.

When seedlings are 2.5cm high, this out to give the beetroot space.

Add 5 cm of rich organic matter or compost to soil and plant bell pepper plant 30cm apart from each other. Make sure that the plant is in direct sunlight and water soil to bed in plant.

Water Requirements

Water plant every other day.

Yeild:

1 Beetroot per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Pull up alternate plants once they have reached golf ball size to use as a tasty treat in the kitchen, leaving the others to reach maturity. Harvest these when they are the size of a cricket ball.

Storing Guidlines

The root bulbs should also be bagged and can be stored in the refrigerator crisper drawer 7 to 10 days.
