Brussel Sprouts

Soil Requirements

Brussel Sprouts require a slightly acidic soil of between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Brussel Sprouts also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 20cm²
Height 60cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow indoors in early February in a cell tray and be sure to keep soil moist. Once plant is 5cm in height, transfer to bigger pot.

When plant has reached 10-15cm, usually in mid-May, transfer plant to the outdoors. Prepare ground by mixing compost with soil and ensure the area you use gets at least 6 hours of sunlight a day. When planting, leave 60cm space between each Brussel sprouts plant and water soil well to bed plant in.

Water Requirements

Once planted outside, water every 10 days with a water and nitrogen rich fertiliser.

Pruning Requirements

Begin pruning Brussels sprouts when you see at least one sprout develop. At this time, prune off the lowest 6-8 leaves. The cut should be as close to the main vertical stem as possible.

Yeild:

30-40 sprouts per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Sprouts can be harvested as early as august but can run all the way through to the following January. The ideal sprout will be tightly closed, firm to touch and the size of a golf ball. Harvest from the bottom of the plant first and work your way up. Take what you need and not more.

Storing Guidlines

Bussel sprouts keep for a week in the refrigerator.
