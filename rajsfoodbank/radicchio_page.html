Radicchio

Soil Requirements

Radicchio require a neutral soil of between 7.5 to 8.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Radicchio also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 15cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Radicchio seeds indoors at the end of April. Use a cell tray filled with compost and germinate one seed per cell. Cover each seed with soil and water well. Leave on widow ceil in full sunshine.

Once seedling is 5cm in height, transfer outdoors. Choose an area that receives direct sunlight of the duration of the day. Prepare the ground by mixing a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil.

Plant seedlings 15cm apart from each other and water in well.

Water Requirements

Water once everyday.

Feed with liquid fertiliser very 14 days.

Start blanching radicchio about 12 weeks after sowing. Block sunlight for 4 days.

Yeild:

1 bulb per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest after the blanching period.

Storing Guidlines

Radicchio should be kept in the refrigerator 1 week.
