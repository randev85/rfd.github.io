Carrots

Soil Requirements

Carrots requires a slightly acidic soil of between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Carrots also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 5cm²
Height 20cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow carrot seeds outdoors in the month of February. Ensure the spot you choose gets direct sunlight for the duration of the day. Prepare the ground by adding 7cm of rich organic matter or compost to the soil. Loosen the ground as carrots like space to grow. Plant carrot seed at a 1cm depth in the soil and leave 5cm of space between each seed.

Water Requirements

Water every 10-14 days.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any yellow or dying leaves.

Yeild:

1 Carrot per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Carrots are ready for harvesting about 12-16 weeks after sowing.

Storing Guidlines

Remove all the green stubble to prevent the carrot from rotting. Allow the carrot outer skin to dry in sun for a day or so. Do not wash until ready to use carrot. Place carrots into refrigerator. Carrots can keep in the refrigerator for up to three months.
