Shallots

Soil Requirements

Shallots require a neutral soil of between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Shallots also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 15cm²
Height 20cm

Additional Information

Cover with net to stop birds removing sets.

Seed Germination & Planting

Plant shallot sets outdoors from mid-November to mid-March. Choose an area that gets direct sunlight for most of the day. Prepare ground by mixing in a 7cm layer of rich organic matter or compost to the soil. Gently push shallot set into soft, well-worked soil so that the tip is just showing and firm the soil around them. Leave 25cm between each set of shallots.

Water Requirements

Shallots require watering every other day.

Feed soil with a nitrogen rich liquid every 2 weeks.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any flower spikes as soon as they are seen.

Yeild:

12 bulbs per set.

Harvesting Guidelines

Shallots are ready to harvest when the foliage starts to turn yellow in July.

Storing Guidlines

Shallots should be stored in a cool, dry, well-ventilated space out of direct sunlight.
