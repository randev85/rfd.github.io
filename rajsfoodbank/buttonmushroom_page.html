Button Mushrooms

Growing Conditions

Button mushrooms grow best on a combination of saw dust, wood chip, grain and straw. Add water to this and this is good material for spores to grow off.

Mushrooms prefer dark, cool, moist, and humid growing environments. It is important the temperature stays between 13-21’C for the ideal growing conditions.

Space Requirements

Area Use a 30x20cm tray.
Height 3-5cm

Additional Information

It's probably best to place your growing tray under you sink.

Growing Spores

Mix spores with saw dust and grain mix to create spawn. Place the spawn in a tray and the tray over a heat pad for three weeks. Sustain the temperature 21’C during these three weeks until you see the mycelium forming. mycelium are the tiny, threadlike roots. Reduce the heat pad’s temperature to 13’C and cover saw dust and grain mix with an inch of fertile soil.

Water Requirements & Fertiliser Requirements

Keep the soil moist by spraying it with water and covering it with a damp cloth. Ensure cloth stay damp all the time by spraying with water when required.

Yeild:

Harvesting every day should result in a continuous crop for about six months.

Harvesting Guidelines

Button mushrooms should appear within three to four weeks. Harvest them when the caps open and the stalk can be cut with a sharp knife from the stem. Avoid pulling up the mushrooms, or you risk damage to surrounding fungi that are still developing.

Storing Guidlines

Store Mushrooms in the fridge for 1-2 weeks.
