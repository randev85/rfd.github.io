Artichoke

Soil Requirements

Artichokes require a slightly acidic soil of between 6.0 to 7.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Artichokes also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 90cm²
Height 1m

Seed Germination & Planting

Germinate artichoke seeds at the start of march indoors. Use a small plastic pot with holes at the bottom of it for good drainage. Place compost enriched soil in pot and be sure not to compact down too much. Plant seeds 2.5cm below the surface of the soil and initially water without letting soil become soggy. From here on in, only water the pot so that the soil remains barely moist.

Place you pots on a window ceil that gets plenty of light and in an area that maintains an average temperature of 20’C.

The artichoke seed will take between 2-3 weeks to sprout. Once sprouted, leave for a week before placing sprouts in their own pots and feeding with a weak fertilising water once a week.

The plant should grow 25-30cm in height in the space of 2-3 months whilst being indoors. At this stage it’s ready to be transferred outside.

Mix a 5cm layer of compost with the outdoor soil before planting. Plant each artichoke 70cm apart from each other and in an area that get plenty of sunny light.

Water Requirements

Once planted outside, water every other day.

Pruning Requirements

Allow leaves to fully spread and grow. Only cut away the dying leave.

Yeild:

12 buds per plant on average

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest artichoke flower buds whilst they are closed, tight and firm, and have a diameter of 5-7cm. Cut bud 3 inches down the stem to provide you with a handle. Buds first start to appear at the end of July and the plant is then able to produce a 2ndharvest all the way into August/September. After the first harvest, prune away the plant by two thirds of its size and this encourages better growth for it’s second harvest.

Storing Guidlines

Place artichoke in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.
