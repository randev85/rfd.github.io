Garlic

Soil Requirements

Garlic requires a neutal soil of between 6.0 to 7.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Garlic also requires a loam textured soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 30cm

Additional Information

Garlic can be planted at 2 times in the year.

Seed Germination & Planting

Plant garlic clove directly in the soil at a depth 2.5cm and leave 15cm between each bulb. Ensure the flattened bottom of the bulb is facing down.

Plant bulb in late October or early March. This is because the cloves require a two month period of temperatures of between 0-10’C for good development.

Water thoroughly after planting.

Water Requirements

Keep moist by watering once every other day and once a day during hot spells.

Pruning Requirements

Remove dry or dying leaves.

Yeild:

1 clove will produce 1 bulb.

Harvesting Guidelines

If you’ve planted your garlic in late October, harvest at the end of June. If you’ve planted your garlic in march, harvest at the end of August.

Remove bulb from soil gently with a fork and cut off excess stem for the compost bin.

Storing Guidlines

Leave to hang in a dry place at a temperature of about 16'C. Garlic bulbs have a storage life of about 3-5 months.
