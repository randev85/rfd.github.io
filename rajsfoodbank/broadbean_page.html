Broad Beans

Soil Requirements

Broad beans plants requires a neutral soil of 7.0ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Broad beans plants also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 20cm²
Height 1.2m

Additional Information

Use a ridgid stick and ties to support the growth of plant.

Seed Germination & Planting

Broad beans can be either sown in November or February in direct soil. Prepare soil by mixing in a 5cm layer of rich organic material or compost with it. Use an area that receive a lot of sunshine. Plant seeds at a 7cm depth in the soil and leave a 20cm gap between each seed. Water soil well when bedding in seeds.

Water Requirements

Water plant every other day.

Once plant starts to flower, water once a day.

Pruning Requirements

The broad bean plant requires very little pruning. Just remove any yellow or dying leaves.

Yeild:

10-15 pods on average per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest pods once beans have begun to visibly swell inside. Harvest plants in stages, starting with the lowest pod first; small beans are sweeter and more tender that large ones. Pods can also be picked when they are immature to be cooked and eaten whole.

Storing Guidlines

Store in the fridge once picked for 1 week.
