Habanero

Soil Requirements

Habanero pepper plants requires a slightly acidic soil of between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Habanero plants also requires semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 20cm²
Height 60-90cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Habanero seeds at the start of April and keep indoors to germinate. Plant seeds in small compost pots and keep in a propagator at 20â€™C in temperature. Place pots at window ceil and be sure to keep soil moist.

Keep plants indoors for the duration of 4 weeks before transferring outdoors.

Add 5 cm of compost to soil and plant habanero 30cm apart from each other. Make sure that the plant is in direct sunlight and water soil to bed in plant.

Water Requirements

Water plant every other day.

Once plant starts to produce peppers, water once a week to produce hotter peppers.

Pruning Requirements

Once the plant is 20cm in height and has at least 12 leaves, prune the plant to two main branches. Select the two healthiest branches.

Clip out the growth tips of each shoot, after the second leaf, to encourage branching. Stems grow sturdier and plants bush up when pruned early in the season.

Remove any yellow leaves and put in the compost bin.

The fewer pepper you have fruiting, the bigger and spicier the peppers will be. Pinch out new flower buds to prevent the plant from spending energy on further fruit set.

Yeild:

20-30 peppers per plant on average

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest habanero peppers at the end of August or until they have fully ripened to an orange/red colour.

Storing Guidlines

Wash and pat dry before placing in a sealable bag and in the freezer.
