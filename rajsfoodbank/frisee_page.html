Frisée

Soil Requirements

Frisée requires a slightly acidic soil of between 5.5 to 7.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Frisée also requires semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 30cm²
Height 10cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Frisée seeds can be sown as early as the end of February to July. Place seeds in small compost pots and in a propagator at a temperature of 18-22’C. Ensure you keep the soil moist during this sprouting period. Keep in propagator for about a month.

Transfer plant from compost pot to soil from April to August. Place the sprout at a depth of 1 cm in the soil and leave 30 cm between each sprout.

Water Requirements

Water plant once a day to keep the soil moist. Be sure not to waterlog soil.

Pruning Requirements

Remove dying leaves.

Yeild:

400-500g per head of Frisée

Harvesting Guidelines

Sever the head with a sharp knife when the leaves are creamy white.

Harvest ‘cut and come again’ leaves with scissors.

Storing Guidlines

Pick, wash and spin-dry leaves before placing in the fridges. Consume with a week of picking.
