Spring Onion

Soil Requirements

Spring Onions require an acidic soil of between 5.3 to 5.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Spring Onions also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 15cm²
Height 30cm

Additional Information

Cover with net to stop birds removing sets.

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Spring onions outdoors from the start of March to the middle of September. Choose an area that gets direct sunlight for most of the day. Prepare ground by mixing in a 7cm layer of rich organic matter or compost to the soil. Sow seeds at a depth of 1cm in the soil and leave 10cm between each seed.

Water Requirements

Spring onions require watering every other day.

Feed soil with a nitrogen rich liquid every 4 weeks.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any flower spikes as soon as they are seen.

Yeild:

1 spring onion per bulb

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest when spring onion is 15cm.

If you've sown your seeds at the end of the growing season, harvest in March in the following year.

Storing Guidlines

Spring onions should be kept in the refrigerator 1-2 weeks.
