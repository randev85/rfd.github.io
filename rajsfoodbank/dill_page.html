Dill

Soil Requirements

Dill requires an acidic soil of between 5.5 to 6.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Dill also requires semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 30cm²
Height 60-120cm

Additional Information

Support dill stems with twigs to stop them being blown down by the wind. Don't plant dill near fennel as they will cross pollinate

Seed Germination & Planting

The dill plant doesn’t transfer well as it roots dislike being disturbed. Therefore, it is best to plant directly in a pot or in the ground.

Prepare the soil by mixing in 5 cm of compost to it. Plant seeds at a 1cm depth and with 15cm distance between each seed. Ensure growing site gets the full sun.

Water Requirements

Water plant daily to ensure soil doesn’t dry out. In the summer months, water in the early morning or late evening.

Pruning Requirements

If you only require the dill leaves and not the buds, pinch out the top buds and this will encourage a bushier plant. Otherwise, just remove dying leaves.

Yeild:

400g per plant on Average

Harvesting Guidelines

Leaves can be harvested as soon as two weeks from planting. Cut leaves when required and if picked regularly, this will help the plant’s productivity.

Storing Guidlines

Dill leaves can be kept in the fridge for 1 week, frozen or dried out and kept in an air-tight container for a longer period.
