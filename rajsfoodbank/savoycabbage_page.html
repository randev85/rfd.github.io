Savoy Cabbage

Soil Requirements

Cabbage requires a neutral soil of between 6.5 to 7.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Cabbage also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Wherever possible, prepare the soil in autumn by adding well-rotted manure or garden compost and then leave it over winter to consolidate.

Space Requirements

Area 30cm²
Height 20cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Cabbage sowing times vary depending upon the harvest times. Spring cabbage should be sown in in July/August and transplanted to the ground in September/August. Summer cabbage should be sown in late February/early March and transplanted to the ground in May/June. Winter cabbage should be sown in April/May and transplanted to the ground in June/July.

Any seeds sown in late February should be kept under a cloche until early May.

Sow Cabbage seeds outdoors between the months of February to September. Sow in cell tray filled with moist compost, one seed per cell. Choose an area that receives direct sunlight of the duration of the day.

Water the plant well the day before transferring to the ground.

Prepare the ground by mixing a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil. Once the seedling is big enough to handle, transfer to the soil and leave 30-45cm of space in between each cabbage. Water well.

Water Requirements

When the heads begin to form, generous watering will greatly improve head size. Feed summer and winter cabbages with a high-nitrogen fertiliser before they get too big.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves.

Yeild:

1 Cabbage per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Cabbages are harvested by cutting through the stem just above ground level with a sharp knife. Cut a 1cm deep cross in the stump of spring and summer cabbages and you'll be rewarded with a second crop of much smaller cabbages.

Storing Guidlines

If cabbage is properly stored, it can last from 3 weeks to up to 2 months in your refrigerator.
