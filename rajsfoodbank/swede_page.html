Swede

Soil Requirements

Swede requires a neutral soil of between 7.0 to 7.4 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Swede also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 20cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow swede seeds outdoors between early May and the middle of June. Choose an area that receives direct sunlight of the duration of the day. Prepare the ground by mixing a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil. Sow seeds at a depth of 2cm in the soil and leave 23cm between each seed.

Water Requirements

Keep the soil moist to ensure rapid growth, keep the roots fleshy and tasty and prevent splitting.

Yeild:

1 Swede per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Lift roots from September to November once large enough to use, or leave in the ground until Christmas. Cover with straw or bracken before the onset of cold weather.

Storing Guidlines

If you're planning to consume your swede within a week, remove greens from the swede, and place in a bowl of water. Place bowl of water in the fridge and the turnips will stay fresh for 1-2 weeks.
