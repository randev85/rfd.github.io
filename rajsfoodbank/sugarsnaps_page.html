Sugar Snaps

Soil Requirements

Sugar Snaps require a slightly acidic soil of between 5.8 to 7.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Sugar Snaps also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 25cm²
Height 90cm

Additional Information

Add a trellis behind the plant. This will support the plant.

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Sugar Snap seeds outdoors at the beginning of April. Choose an area that receives full sun shine for the duration of the day. Prepare ground by mixing in a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost into the soil. Make a trench thatâ€™s 5cm deep and 15cm wide. Sow seeds 7.5cm apart from each other and cover with soil. Water in well.

Water Requirements

Water well once a week.

After flowering, plants need sufficient water for the pods to swell properly. Check the soil moisture at root level to find out if the plants are getting enough water.

Yeild:

1kg per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Sugar Snap peas should be picked when the pods are about 7.5cm long, just as the peas are starting to develop.

Storing Guidlines

Sugar Snaps should be kept in the refrigerator 1 week.
