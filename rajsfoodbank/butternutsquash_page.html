Butternut Squash

Soil Requirements

Butternut Squash requires a slightly acidic soil the ranges between 6.0 to 6.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Butternut Squash also requires a semi loose rich soil that is free from weeds and roots.

Space Requirements

Area Up to 4.5m Vines
Height 10cm

Additional Information

Vines can grow outwards to cover 4.5m.

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow butternut squash seeds outdoors in late May/early June. Sow directly in ground and use an area that receives direct sun light for most of the day. Prepare ground by mixing in 5cm of rich organic material or compost with the soil. Plant the seeds at a depth of 2.5cm in the ground and leave 10cm of space in between each seed. Water soil well before covering growing area with a cloche. Remove cloche after 4 weeks once the shouts are 15cm in length.

Water Requirements & Fertiliser Requirements

Keep the soil constantly moist by watering around the plants, not over them.

Feed plant with rich liquid fertiliser every 10-14 days once the plant has began to fruit.

Support fruit off the ground by placing it on a tile.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying or yellow leaves.

Yeild:

10 Squash on average per vine.

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest fruit at the end of October when skin turns hard and is difficult to pierce with your thumbnail. The butternut squash should be at least 15cm in length.

Storing Guidlines

The squash keep longer if you can slow the respiration rate. This may be done by lowering the temperature.
