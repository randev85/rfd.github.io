Celeriac

Soil Requirements

Celeriac requires a slightly acidic soil of between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Celeriac also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 5cm²
Height 20cm

Additional Information

Cover with a thick layer of bracken or straw during the cold winter months to prevent the ground from freezing.

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Celeriac seeds in cell tray and in a propagator at 15-18’C. Do this at the start of march and place on a window ceil in full sunshine.

Once the seedling has grown enough to handle, transfer to a pot and keep at 15-18’C. Ensure that this temperature is maintained as excessive cold can lead to premature flowering.

Transfer to the outdoors at the start of June. Prepare the ground by mixing in rich organic matter or compost into the soil. Choose an area that receives full sunshine. Plant celeriac 30cm apart from each other

Water Requirements

Water every day as Celeriac is a moisture-loving plant.

Pruning Requirements

As the plants mature, remove the outer leaves as they fall horizontally, to expose the crown and allow it to develop. Remove side shoots if they appear.

Yeild:

1 Celeriac per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest from October to the following March.

Storing Guidlines

Leave in the ground until required.
