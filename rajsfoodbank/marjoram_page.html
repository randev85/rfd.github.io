Marjoram

Soil Requirements

Marjoam requires a neutral soil the ranges between 6.7 to 7.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Marjoram also requires a semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 30cm²
Height 20cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow marjoram seed indoors from mid-February. Fill a small pot with compost and sow 2-3 seed on the surface of each pot. Water well and place pot in a propagator.

Once seedlings are big enough to handle, separate them to their own pots with fresh soil/compost and ensure the soil stays moist.

Allow plant to grow indoors until the start of June before transferring to the outdoors. Prepare ground by mixing a 5cm layer of compost with the soil. Choose an area the receives 5-6 hours of sunlight a day. Place each plant 30cm apart from each other. Water the soil well after transfer to the outdoors.

Water & Fertiliser Requirements

Water every other day to keep the soil moist but avoid over watering.

Feed plants weekly with balanced liquid fertiliser.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves/branches from the plant.

Yeild:

400g on average per plant per year.

Harvesting Guidelines

Pick leaves before the flower buds open.

Storing Guidlines

Either use fresh, or dry and freeze for use later.
