Fennel

Soil Requirements

Fennel requires a neutal soil of between 6.5 to 7.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Fennel also requires a fine and loose textured soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 20cm²
Height 30cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow seeds directly in to soil as root system doesnâ€™t do well when moved. Sow at the end of February/start of March. When planting, set seed at a 1cm depth in the soil and leave a 25cm space between each seed.

Water Requirements

It is imperative that you keep the roots of the fennel bulb quenched with water. Water 1-2 times a day but be sure not to waterlog the soil.

Pruning Requirements

Allow the fennel bulb to grow naturally and only remove dead leaves.

Yeild:

1 bulb per seed

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest fennel bulb 12-14 weeks from planting the seed. Pick the leaves and keep the bulb before discarding stalk, flowers and roots to the compost bin.

Storing Guidlines

Fennel leaves can be kept in the fridge for 1 week, frozen or dried out and kept in an air-tight container for a longer period.

Store bulb in the fridge for 1-2 weeks.
