Bean Sprouts

Seed Germination

Sterilise a large glass jar with boiling hot water. You can use just about any whole or dried bean and legume or seed for sprouting. This includes chickpeas, alfalfa, adzuki beans, mung beans, lentils, sunflower seeds or quinoa.

For beans and legumes, fill jar a quarter of the way up and wash and rinse in cold water 2-3 times until water runs clear.

For seeds, use about 5g per jar before washing and rinsing.

Cover beans or seeds with fresh water and place a piece of muslin over the rim before securing with rubber band.

Leave to soak over night.

Remove muslin and drain of water. Rinse beans or seeds with fresh water before draining again and reapplying the muslin. Ensure there isnâ€™t any excess water in the jar as this could lead to mould or bacterial growth.

Store beans or seeds out of direct sunlight and at room temperature.

Water Requirements

Once the beans or seeds start to sprout, wash and rinse every 8-12 hours with fresh water.

Yeild:

200-300g depending on the size of the jar.

Harvesting Guidelines

When the sprouts have reached the desired length, remove the muslin and transfer the sprouts to a colander. Rinse them with clean water, and leave them to drip dry for a few minutes, or pat them dry with a clean towel.

Storing Guidlines

Sprouts will keep in the refrigerator for two to three days
