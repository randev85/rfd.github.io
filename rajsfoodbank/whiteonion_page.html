White Onion

Soil Requirements

Onions require a slightly acidic soil of between 5.5 to 6.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Onions also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 20cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Onion seeds indoors in the middle of February. Sow in cell tray using one seed per cell. Place seeds at a 1cm depth in the soil and place tray on a window ceil. Ensure soil stay moist during germination.

Transfer to the outdoors once the seedling has reached 10cm in height. Choose an area that gets partial sunlight. Prepare ground by mixing a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil. Plant onion bulbs at a depth of 3cm and leaves 15cm between each plant.

Water Requirements

Onions requires watering every other day.

Feed soil with a nitrogen rich liquid every 2 weeks.

Yeild:

1 Onion per stem

Harvesting Guidelines

Onions can be harvested when the foliage turns yellow and starts to topple over.

Storing Guidlines

Onions should be stored in a cool, dry, well-ventilated space out of direct sunlight.
