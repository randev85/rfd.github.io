Spinach

Soil Requirements

Spinach requires a neutral soil the ranges between 6.5 to 7.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Spinach also requires a semi loose rich soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 15cm²
Height 20cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow spinach seeds outdoors from the start of March to May. Prepare the ground by mixing 5cm of rich organic matter or compost with the soil. Choose an area that get a lot of sunshine.

sow seeds at a 2.5cm depth in the soil and leave 15cm of space between each seed. If you are planting multiple rows, leaves 30cm between each row.

The plant will need insulation after september too stay alive. Doing this by either adding a layer of mulches or straw to the ground.

Water & Fertiliser Requirements

Water every other day to keep the soil moist. Water once in the morning everyday in the summer months.

Feed plants every 2 weeks with balanced liquid fertiliser.

To prevent the leaves tasting bitter make sure the soil is rich and contains plenty of organic matter.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves from the plant.

Yeild:

100-150g on average per plant per year.

Harvesting Guidelines

Either pick between late May and the end of October or between October and April.

Harvest the leaves continually once they're large enough to pick.

Storing Guidlines

Pick, wash and use when needed.
