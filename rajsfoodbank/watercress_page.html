Watercress

Soil Requirements

Watercress requires a neutral soil the ranges between 6.5 to 7.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Watrecress also requires a semi loose rich soil that is free from weeds and roots.

Space Requirements

Area 8cm²
Height 10cm

Additional Information

Grow in plastic pot and place pot in water.

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow watercress crops outdoors in plastic pots with drainage from March to April. Place seeds at a 1cm depth of the soil and be sure to keep the soil moist during the germination period.

Once the seedling have sprouted, place the pots in a deep tray and add water to submerge the pots half way up.

Place tray in direct sunlight.

Water Requirements & Fertiliser Requirements

Change water in tray every week and dilute with liquid fertiliser every two weeks.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves.

Harvesting Guidelines

Cut plants regularly while they are fresh and young, as this will encourage further growth.

Storing Guidlines

Pick wash and use when required.
