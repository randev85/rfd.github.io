Baby Gem Lettuce

Soil Requirements

Baby gem lettuce require a neutral soil of between 6.5 to 7.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Baby gem lettuce also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 15cm

Additional Information

Baby gem lettuce is susceptible to slugs and snails. Copper tape or slug barriers are a great repellent against an infestation.

Seed Germination & Planting

Germinate iceberg lettuce seeds in the last week of February indoors. Place seeds in small compost pots with soil rich in organic matter. Ensure the soil stays moist and the seed is kept in a propagator at 18-20’C.

Allow the seed to sprout indoor for the duration of four weeks.

Before transferring plant outside, prepare the ground with 5cm of organic matter or compost. Choose an area that gets about 5 hours of sunshine per day. Create 5cm deep cavity in the soil and transfer lettuce plant from compost pot to it. Push soil around base of the plant and water well. Leave 10cm space between each baby gem lettuce.

Water Requirements

Baby gem lettuce requires watering once a day.

Feed soil with a nitrogen rich liquid every 2 weeks.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any yellow or dying leaves.

Yeild:

1 head of lettuce per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Baby gem lettuce should be harvested when it’s reached full size but just before maturity. This will normally be done in the third to fourth week of July.

Storing Guidlines

Store in the fridge for 1-2 weeks and wash before consumption.
