Lemongrass

Soil Requirements

Lemongrass can tolerate a soil the ranges between 5.0 to 8.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Lemongrass also requires a semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 10cm²
Height 90cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow lemongrass seeds in early March in small cell tray. Place seed at a 5mm depth of surface and ensure the soil is always moist. Place cell tray in a propagator.

Once the seedling is big enough to handle, transfer to individual pots that a bigger and apply a 2cm layer of compost to soil. Keep soil moist.

When the roots start to show, transfer to a bigger pot of a size of 20cm in diameter. Apply fresh layer of compost and water in well.

Transfer plant to the outdoors at the start of June. Ensure the area chosen gets direct sunlight and sheltered from the wind. Plant lemongrass 15cm apart from each other.

Water & Fertiliser Requirements

Water once a day in the morning to keep the soil moist but avoid over watering.

Feed plants weekly with balanced liquid fertiliser.

Pruning Requirements

When foliage starts to turn brown in autumn, cut back foliage to 10cm.

Yeild:

300g on average per plant per year.

Harvesting Guidelines

Plants can be harvested all year round. Either cut entire stems as necessary, trimming off leaves and retaining the bottom 8-10cm.

Storing Guidlines

Pick, wash and consume when needed.
