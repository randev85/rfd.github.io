Radish

Soil Requirements

Radish requires a slightly acidic soil of between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Radish also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 5cm²
Height 15cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow radish outdoors from March to mid-August. Sow seed in the spot where it is to grow. Choose an area that receives direct sunlight of the duration of the day. Prepare the ground by mixing a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil. Sow seed at a depth of 1cm and leave 2.5cm between each seedling before watering in well.

Sow summer cultivators between March and June for harvest in May to November. Sow winter cultivators between July and August for harvest in December to February.

Water Requirements

Keep the soil moist to ensure rapid growth, keep the roots fleshy and tasty and prevent splitting. Sowing in July and August can be a problem due to the drier conditions, so make sure these wtered well in the morning everyday.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves.

Yeild:

1 Radish per seed.

Harvesting Guidelines

Radish are ready as early as 3 weeks after sowing. Radishes need to be grown steadily and harvested young to ensure they remain succulent, otherwise they can become woody and inedible.

Storing Guidlines

If you're planning to consume your radishes within a week, remove greens from the radish, and place in a bowl of water. Place bowl of water in the fridge and the radishes will stay fresh for 1-2 weeks.
