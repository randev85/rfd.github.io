Swiss Chard

Soil Requirements

Swiss Chard requires a neutral soil the ranges between 6.0 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Swiss Chard also requires a semi loose rich soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 25cm²
Height 30cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow Swiss chard seed indoors from March to July. Sow in cell tray with rich composted soil by placing seed at a 1cm depth. Ensure the soil stay moist during this germinating period. Once the seedling is big enough to handle, separate them to their own pots with fresh soil/compost and ensure the soil stays moist.

The plant can be transfer to the outdoors as early as the start of May. Choose an area with open sunlight to plant chard. Prepare the ground by mixing in 5cm of compost to the soil before planting chard seedlings 30cm apart from each other.

The plant will need insulation after september too stay alive. Doing this by either adding a layer of mulches or straw to the ground.

Water & Fertiliser Requirements

Water every other day to keep the soil moist. Water once in the morning everyday in the summer months.

Feed plants every 2 weeks with balanced liquid fertiliser.

To prevent the leaves tasting bitter make sure the soil is rich and contains plenty of organic matter.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves from the plant.

Yeild:

200-300g per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Cut off the outer leaves first when they are young and tender, working towards the centre. Don't wait until they reach maximum size.

Harvest regularly to ensure a constant supply of tender re-growth.

Storing Guidlines

Pick, wash and use when needed.
