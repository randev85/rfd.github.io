Tomato

Soil Requirements

Tomatoes require a slightly acidic soil of between 6.2 to 6.8 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Tomatoes also require semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good drainage.

Space Requirements

Area 40cm²
Height 1.5m

Additional Information

Once the flowers begin to fruit, it is important to support the stems with rigid sticks to support the weight.

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow tomato seeds indoors at the start of February. Place seeds in cell trays filled with moist compost and place the tray in a propagator. Sustain temperature at 18â€™C and place propagator on window ceil.

Once plant has produced to leaves, transplant to a 9cm small pot, add fresh compost and water in well.

Transfer plant to the outdoors at the end of May. Choose an area that receives direct sunlight through out the whole day. Prepare the ground by mixing a 7cm layer of organic matter or compost with the soil. Leave at least 45cm between each plant and make sure the plant is watered in well.

Use a ridged stick and ties to support the main stem of the plant whilst it grows.

Water Requirements

Water regularly to keep the soil/compost evenly moist. Irregular watering, together with a lack of calcium in the soil leads to blossom end rot - the bottom of the fruit turns black and becomes sunken. Feed every 10-14 days with a balanced liquid fertiliser, changing to a high potash one once the first fruits start to set.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any dying leaves.

Remove the sideshoots regularly when they are about 2.5cm long. This will lead to more flowers and therefore, more fruit.

Yeild:

20-30 tomatoes per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest fruit once they are fully ripe and vibrant red or yellow depending on the type of tomato.

Storing Guidlines

Once picked, do not place in the refrigerator. They need to stay at room temperature and out of direct sunlight. Store them stem side down while they finish ripening.
