Coriander

Soil Requirements

Coriander require a neutral soil of between 6.5 to 7.5 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Coriander also requires a semi loose soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 12cm²
Height 40cm

Seed Germination & Planting

Sow coriander seeds outdoor at the start of June. Mix a 5cm layer of compost with soil and place coriander seed at a 1cm depth. Leave a 10cm gap between each seed and make sure the area gets at least 4 hours of sunshine a day.

Water Requirements

Keep the soil moist but avoid over watering. Coriander will flower prematurely if the soil gets too dry.

Pruning Requirements

Keep plants bushy and productive by pinching the tips of branches regularly and remove any flowers that start to develop. If the plant is allowed to flower, it will lose flavor

Yeild:

340-360g on average per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Start picking leaves in July as required. Pick leaves from the top to encourage a bushier plant.

Storing Guidlines

Pick, wash and consume when needed.
