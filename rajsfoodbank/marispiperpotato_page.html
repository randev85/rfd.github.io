Maris Piper Potato

Soil Requirements

Potatoes require an acidic soil of between 5.0 to 6.0 ph. Test your soil with a soil ph meter and if required, add alkaline by either using lime, mulches or a bicarbonate of soda solution (1 tsp per 2 litres of water). Alternatively, if there is large concentrations of alkaline in the soil, treat with a vinegar solution (1 tablespoon per 2 litres) until ph levels are right.

Potatoes also require semi firm soil that is free from weeds and roots. This is because the plant needs good water retention in the soil.

Space Requirements

Area 30cm²
Height 20cm

Additional Information

Earthing up protects developing new potatoes from light and can stop the potato becoming green and poisonous.

Seed Germination & Planting

Plant potato tubers from the end of March to the end of April. It is essential to let the tubers start to sprout before planting in the ground. Choose an area that receives 4-5 hours of sunlight a day. Prepare the ground by mixing in a 5cm layer of rich organic matter or compost with the soil. Dig a narrow trench to a depth of 12cm. Line trench with grass or extra compost and place tubers 25cm apart from each other. Cover potatoes with 10cm of soil.

Once the stem is 23cm in height, carefully draw up soil 15cm up the stem to create a mound.

Water Requirements

Potato requires watering every other day.

Feed soil with a nitrogen rich liquid every 2 weeks.

Pruning Requirements

Remove any yellow or dying leaves.

Yeild:

1.5-2kg per plant.

Harvesting Guidelines

Harvest potatoes 3-3.5 months after planting tuber.

Storing Guidlines

Keep potatoes in a dry, dark place that is well-ventilated. A wicker basket kept in a kitchen cupboard should do the trick. Be sure your potatoes are kept in cool conditions as it increases storage length.
